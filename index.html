Schematic

Keshiki (景色 — scenery) is a philosophical IoT device for people with learned helplessness. The name comes from Kikuo's concept in the film 国宝: that beauty is earned through suffering and growth, not given.

A dome enclosing a small articulated humanoid robot. The robot's posture and the dome's internal light shift in response to the user's emotional state, tracked via AI — beginning dim, becoming luminous over time. The AI acts not as a teacher but as a quiet companion that grows alongside the user.

The spine is the physical form the humanoid takes: anatomically-informed PETG vertebrae with living-hinge joints, a wire running through the neural foramina to an LED tip. SG90 servo + ESP32 controlled.

Mechanical Clock

3D printingPETGFusion 360Mechanical assemblyThingiverse Clock20

Work in progress

A fully 3D-printed mechanical clock (Thingiverse Clock20) in translucent PETG. 135 floating print islands diagnosed by parsing STL geometry directly; solved by adding a base disc in the slicer. PETG stringing fixed via retraction tuning. Steel pin axles hand-cut in 1.5mm, 2mm, 3mm; clearances dialled in by feel. A lesson in the gap between digital geometry and physical tolerance.

Straight Lines & Curves

GrasshopperRhinoComputational designDesign theory

Work in progress

A design research project asking: what makes the balance between straight lines and curves visually beautiful? Inspired by Kaede Sugano's methodology.

The Linearity Index: 0 = pure curves, 1 = pure straight lines. Implemented in Grasshopper/Rhino. Three modes: Analysis (curvature heatmap), Comparison (library: Aalto ≈0.05, Eames ≈0.30, Yohji ≈0.40, Braun SK4 ≈0.55), Generation (real-time silhouette deformation via slider + Graph Mapper).
